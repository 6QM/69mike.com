The Worker Investor begins with a practical reality: most people first live as workers. Work provides income, training, experience, responsibility, and contact with real constraints.

This series asks how a person can remain inside that reality while gradually converting wages, skills, knowledge, experience, and public work into assets that continue to create value over time. The goal is not to stop working immediately. It is to build more choice over what work to do, whom to work with, and how to use one's time.

The independent website preserves the project's central proposition. The essays below contain its continuing cases, arguments, experiments, and revisions.
